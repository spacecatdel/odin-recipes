Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a delicious italian dessert consisting of layers of cream, marscarpone and ladyfingers soaked in coffee. The word tiramisu translates to "pick-me-up."

Traditionally, tiramisu contains alcohol, however, it is just as enjoyable without. If you would prefer not to use alcohol, just leave out the rum.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup coffee at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons rum (optional)
  • 2 (3 ounce) packages ladyfinger cookies
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together egg yolks and sugar.
  2. Whisk in milk and cook over medium heat. Stir constantly until it comes to a boil.
  3. Boil for one minute, then allow to cool.
  4. Cover the mixture and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour.
  5. Whisk the cream and vanilla with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.
  6. Whisk together the egg mixture from the refrigerator and the marscarpone cheese until smooth.
  7. Combine coffee and rum in a small bowl. Briefly soak ladyfingers in the coffee and arrange them to cover the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Spread the half marscarope mixture evenly on top of the ladyfingers. Do the same with the whipped cream.
  9. Repeat the last 2 steps. The tiramisu should have 2 layers of ladyfingers, cheese and cream.
  10. Dust cocoa powder over the top.
  11. Refrigerate for 4 to 6 hours and serve.
